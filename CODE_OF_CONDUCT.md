# Portavoz Community Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

Portavoz is built for people with different languages, backgrounds, abilities,
and levels of experience. Contributors, maintainers, and community members are
expected to make participation welcoming, respectful, and free from harassment.

## Expected behavior

- Be respectful of different viewpoints and experiences.
- Give and receive technical feedback constructively.
- Focus criticism on the work, never on the person.
- Respect privacy: do not publish another person's identity, meeting content,
  recordings, transcripts, or private correspondence without clear permission.
- Assume good intent while remaining accountable for impact.
- Accept responsibility, apologize, and repair harm when appropriate.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, threats, intimidation, discrimination, or personal attacks.
- Sexualized language, imagery, or unwanted attention.
- Trolling, sustained disruption, or knowingly spreading false information.
- Publishing private information or private meeting material without consent.
- Retaliation against someone who reports a concern in good faith.
- Any conduct that would be inappropriate in a professional community.

## Scope

This code applies in Portavoz repositories, discussions, issues, pull requests,
community spaces, and when someone represents the project publicly.

## Reporting and enforcement

For an ordinary moderation concern, contact the maintainer privately at
`security@portavoz.app`. Do not open a public issue containing sensitive
details. Security vulnerabilities should use GitHub's private vulnerability
reporting or the process in [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md).

The maintainer will review reports fairly, preserve confidentiality as far as
practical, and may remove content, close participation, issue a warning, or
temporarily or permanently restrict a participant. Knowingly false or abusive
reports may themselves violate this code. Retaliation is not tolerated.

## Attribution

This policy is informed by the [Contributor Covenant 3.0](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/3/0/)
and adapted to Portavoz's privacy-first community.
